Kimchi



PREP TIME : 2 Hours

TOTAL TIME : 3 Days

YIELDS : 5-6 Cups

Making kimchi at home is the best way to bring the vibrant flavors of Korean cuisine to your table. This tangy and spicy dish is delicious and packed with probiotics, making it great for your gut health. It’s a simple, satisfying way to enjoy a classic Korean favorite and it makes everything taste better from rice bowls to noodles and barbecue!

Go To Recipe

Kimchi



Making kimchi at home is the best way to bring the vibrant flavors of Korean cuisine to your table. This tangy and spicy dish is delicious and packed with probiotics, making it great for your gut health. It’s a simple, satisfying way to enjoy a classic Korean favorite and it makes everything taste better from rice bowls to noodles and barbecue!

Ingredients And Preparation



To start, you’ll need a head of napa cabbage, which forms the base of your kimchi. Begin by cutting the cabbage into quarters, removing the core, and slicing it into smaller, bite-sized pieces. Rinse it really well to remove any dirt or bugs that may have gotten into the cabbage. Then toss the cabbage with a generous amount of coarse salt. Make sure to massage it into every piece, cover it with water, place a heavy plate on top to press it down, and let it sit for about 1-2 hours. This process helps to soften the cabbage and draw out excess moisture, which is crucial for the right texture in your finished kimchi. After it’s softened, rinse the cabbage thoroughly under cold water to remove the salt, and then drain it well.

While your cabbage is softening, you can prepare the kimchi paste, which is where all the magic happens. Start by making a rice flour paste, combining sweet rice flour with water, and cooking it in a pot over medium heat until it thickens. Allow this paste to cool, as it will be mixed with other flavorful ingredients. For the kimchi paste itself, blend a quarter to a third of a yellow onion with several garlic cloves and a small piece of ginger until smooth.

Once your rice flour paste is cooled, mix it with the garlic, onion, and ginger paste. To this mixture, add white sugar, fish sauce, and gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) to taste. The amount of gochugaru will determine how spicy your kimchi is, so adjust according to your preference. A bit of salt is added for seasoning, rounding out the flavors. Now for the veggies — to your bowl with the prepared napa cabbage, add sliced green onions and julienned Korean radish.

Next, combine the kimchi paste with your veggies. Using your hands (gloves are highly recommended to avoid staining your skin), massage the paste into the cabbage until every piece is thoroughly coated. This step is crucial as it ensures that the flavors penetrate every part of the cabbage.

Transfer the mixture into a clean jar or airtight container, packing it tightly to eliminate air pockets. Leave some space at the top of the jar to accommodate the kimchi’s expansion as it ferments. Seal the jar and let it sit at room temperature for about 1-2 days. The fermentation time will vary depending on your room temperature and how tangy you like your kimchi. I like it only a bit tangy so I leave it out overnight and then pop it in the fridge. Once it’s fermented to your liking, move the jar to the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process.
